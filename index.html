Alan Mathison Turing

23 June 1912 - 7 June 1954

Mathematician, Logician, Cryptanalyst, and Theoritical Biologist

  • Alan Turing was widely considered to be the father of theoritical computer science and artificial intelligence.
  • Turing studied undergraduate from 1931 - 1934 at King's College, Cambridge.
  • From September 1936 to July 1938 Turing spent most of his time studying under Church at Princeton University.
  • During Second World War during worked for Government Code and Cypher School (GC&CS).
  • Turing played a pivotal role in cracking intercepted coded messages that enabled the Allies to defeat the Nazis in many crucial engagements, including the Battle of Atlantic; It has been estimated that this work shortened shortened the war in Europe by many as two to four years
